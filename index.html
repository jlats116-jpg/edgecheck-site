The strategy

Backtests usually skip costs. This shows whether the claimed edge survives spread, slippage and commissions — the #1 reason "profitable" strategies die live.

The Crabel preset mirrors a real published backtest: ~67 trades/yr, ~0.13R per trade — impressive equity curve, stopped beating buy-and-hold after ~3 bps of costs per fill.
Gross edge/trade = win% × (W/L ratio × this) − loss% × this.
1 bp = 0.01%. "Zero-commission" brokers still cost you via spread/PFOF.

Why this matters: a famous test of Toby Crabel's opening-range breakout on 15 years of QQQ minute data showed ~1,300% gross returns — and after adding just 1bp commission + 2bps slippage per fill, it stopped beating buy-and-hold entirely. Small per-trade edges × many trades is exactly the shape that costs destroy. If a video shows you an equity curve and doesn't mention slippage, this calculator is the question to ask.

Why we built this

Most "make money trading" content is monetized by selling you something — a course, a community, a challenge fee. The numbers in the thumbnails are gross, hypothetical, or someone else's. We built EdgeCheck to answer the two questions the videos skip:

  • What will getting funded actually cost me? Prop firms profit when you fail challenges. The math of pass probability × fees is knowable — so know it before you pay.
  • Does this strategy survive costs? Academic research and honest backtesters agree: most published retail edges die once you subtract spread, slippage and commissions.

Everything here runs in your browser, is free, and doesn't require an account. If you use the calculators and decide a challenge or a strategy still makes sense — go in with open eyes.
